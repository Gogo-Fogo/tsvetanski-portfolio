1. Focused Overview
Game Title:
Naruto Shippuden: Ultimate Ninja Blazing
Genre:
Tactical Squad-Based RPG with Gacha elements, Turn-Based Strategy, Character Collector
Platform:
Mobile (Android, iOS) – Designed for portrait mobile play
Target Audience:
● Primary: Dedicated fans of the Naruto and Naruto Shippuden anime/manga series,
seeking immersion in the Shippuden era.
● Secondary: Players of tactical RPGs (squad-based combat and turn-based mechanics),
gacha enthusiasts, and mobile gamers looking for strategic gameplay.
● Age Range: Teen to Adult (13+) due to the anime theme and strategic depth.
Game Theme and Narrative:
● Core Theme: Reliving the narrative of Naruto Shippuden through key story arcs and
battles.
● Narrative Focus: Faithfully adapts the Naruto Shippuden storyline, enabling players to
experience familiar events and character arcs.
● Character-Driven Gameplay: Emphasis on collecting, training, and using a wide array
of Naruto Shippuden characters, where team synergy is vital.

2. Deep Dive into Core Gameplay Mechanics
2.1. Strategic Turn-Based Battle System
● Unique Battlefield: Battles unfold on a tactical sizes and layouts vary to create diverse
scenarios.
● Turn Structure: A traditional turn-based system where players and AI alternate actions.
Each unit can move and act per turn.
● Action Economy: Limited actions per turn (typically one per unit), emphasizing strategic
allocation of movement, attacks, and special skills.

2.2. Unit Movement and Tactical Positioning
● Movement Range: Each ninja has a defined movement range per turn influenced by
stats or abilities.
● Strategic Considerations:
• Engaging Enemies: Positioning within attack range.
• Flanking: Potential for positional advantages.
• Team Attack Setup: Coordinating multiple units to target the same enemy.
• Evading Attacks: Moving out of enemy ranges or avoiding area effects.
• Terrain Utilization: Though not explicitly detailed, terrain (obstacles, cover) likely
affects combat.
2.3. Attack Ranges and Engagement
● Circular Attack Ranges: Standard attacks occur within a circular area around the unit.
● Melee vs. Ranged: Differentiation between close-combat ninjas and those with
longer-range ninjutsu.
● Line of Sight: Likely a factor in determining whether attacks can hit if obstacles or other
units block the view.
2.4. Team Attacks and Synergy
● Triggering Team Attacks: Aligning two or more ninjas to target a single enemy triggers
a team attack.
● Enhanced Damage: Team attacks significantly boost damage compared to individual
strikes.
● Synergy Bonuses: Based on character relationships (e.g., Naruto, Sasuke, Sakura) or

shared affiliations, further enhancing damage or adding effects.
2.5. Devastating Special Attacks (Ninja Jitsu)
● Unique Special Abilities: Each ninja has a signature Ninja Jitsu (e.g., Rasengan,
Chidori) reflecting their anime abilities.
● Irregular Area of Effect: Special attacks target irregular areas (rectangle, cross, cone,
etc.) for strategic impact.
● Charge Mechanic: Special attacks require a charge-up period over several turns.
● Visual Spectacle: Accompanied by impressive animations and effects that enhance the
Naruto theme.
2.6. Elemental Strengths and Weaknesses
● Rock-Paper-Scissors System: A core tactical element based on five elements (Heart,
Skill, Body, Bravery, Wisdom) that likely follow a cyclical advantage system.
● Matchup Advantages: Attacks with elemental advantage deal significantly increased
damage; disadvantageous matchups reduce damage.
● Team Composition Strategy: Players must balance elemental types to counter diverse
enemy compositions.
● Enemy Typing: Enemies are also assigned elemental types, requiring careful analysis
and strategy.
2.7. Shared HP Pool (Risk-Reward System)
● Unified Health: The entire team shares one HP pool rather than individual bars.
● Increased Vulnerability: Damage to any unit reduces the common pool, raising the risk
of a team wipeout.
● Team Survival Emphasis:
• Damage Mitigation: Use of defensive skills and crowd control.
• Healing: Incorporating healing ninjas or abilities.
• Strategic Deployment: Careful placement to minimize overall damage.
● Differentiation: This system sets Blazing apart from traditional RPGs and requires
unique team-building considerations.
2.8. Ninja Borrowing System (Social and Trial Feature)
● Friend/Guest Ninja: At the start of each battle, players may borrow a ninja from a friend
list or as a guest.
● Benefits:
• Trial of New Ninjas: Experience powerful or rare ninjas not in one’s own collection.
• Strategic Advantage: Borrowed ninjas can provide elemental or skill advantages.
• Social Interaction: Encourages adding friends and engaging in the community.
● Limited Use: Borrowed units are available for a single stage or a limited number of uses
per day.

3.1. Engaging Story Mode (Narrative Immersion)
● Anime Story Adaptation: Faithfully recreates the Naruto Shippuden narrative (from
missions like the Kazekage Rescue to the Fourth Great Ninja War).
● Story Presentation: Likely employs visual novel-style cutscenes with dialogues, anime

stills, and in-game cinematics.
● Battle Integration: Key story arcs are interwoven with tactical battles.
● Rewards: Completing story stages yields in-game currency (Ninja Pearls), character
shards, equipment, and unlocks additional content.
3.2. Extensive Character Collection (Gacha Focus)
● Vast Roster: Hundreds of collectible characters from across the Naruto Shippuden
universe, including heroes, villains, and variations (e.g., different Naruto forms).
● Rarity Tiers: Characters are categorized (Common, Rare, Super Rare, Legendary) with
higher rarity units offering superior stats and abilities.
● Summoning (Gacha) System: Players obtain new characters by spending Ninja Pearls;
summoning banners may feature variable rates for specific types or rarities.
● Character Art and Animation: Unique artwork and in-battle animations, with higher
rarity units often featuring moving artwork.

3.3. Character Upgrades and Enhancement Systems
● Leveling: Characters gain EXP from battles to increase levels and base stats (HP,
Attack, Defense).
● Awakening (Evolution): Characters can undergo “Awakening” to boost stats and unlock
new abilities; requires specific materials.
● Limit Breaking: A further upgrade system allowing players to increase the level cap,
acquired through challenging missions (e.g., Trial Missions).
● Skill Enhancement: Active and passive skills can be upgraded to increase
effectiveness.
● Equipment System: Characters can equip items (weapons, armor, accessories) that
boost stats; equipment likely follows its own rarity and upgrade system.
3.4. Diverse Game Modes (Content Variety)

● Story Mode: Detailed narrative campaign as described above.
● Emergency Missions: Limited-time events offering rewards like character shards,
upgrade materials, or exclusive units, often with increased difficulty.
● Trial Missions: Challenges designed to reward players with limit-breaking materials.
● Phantom Castle (PvP Tower Climb): An asynchronous PvP mode where players
ascend a tower by battling other teams, earning rewards based on rank.
● Ninja Road: A mode with sequential maps; clearing all stages unlocks special abilities
for owned characters.
● Daily Missions and Challenges: Daily tasks that reward resources and progression
materials, encouraging regular engagement.

6. Monetization Strategy – Free-to-Play Model Deep Dive
6.1. Free-to-Play Foundation with Optional Purchases
● Accessibility: Free-to-download and play, lowering the entry barrier for Naruto fans.
● Sustainable Revenue: Revenue is generated through optional in-app purchases while
allowing free progression.
6.2. Premium Currency – Ninja Pearls
● Primary Premium Currency: Ninja Pearls are used to summon new characters,
purchase upgrades, and expand character slots.
● Acquisition Methods:

• In-App Purchases: Direct purchase of Ninja Pearls in various pack sizes.
• Gameplay Rewards: Earned through story progression, daily missions, events, and
achievements.
6.3. Gacha Summoning System – Core Monetization Engine
● Random Character Acquisition: Players spend Ninja Pearls for a chance to obtain
characters from a predetermined pool.
● Rarity Rates and Pity System:
• Variable Rarity Rates: Higher rarity characters (e.g., Legendary) are rarer;
summoning banners display these rates.
• Pity System (Speculative): May guarantee a high-rarity character after a certain
number of summons, mitigating prolonged unsuccessful attempts.
● Summoning Banners and Events:
• Regular Banners: Standard summoning pools.
• Event Banners: Limited-time banners (e.g., Blazing Festival) with boosted rates for
specific characters.
● Visual Feedback: The summoning animation (e.g., Naruto breaking gates) provides
cues about the potential rarity of the summoned character.
6.4. Monetization Balance and Player Progression
● Free-to-Play Viability: The game is designed to be accessible to free-to-play players;
early content is available without premium characters.
● Progression Curve and Spending Incentives: The progression becomes steeper at
higher levels or in competitive PvP, making powerful, high-rarity characters more
valuable.
● Value Proposition of Purchases:
• Time Savings: Faster team building compared to relying solely on gameplay
rewards.
• Access to Powerful Characters: Higher chances of obtaining meta-defining ninjas
via summoning banners.
• Competitive Edge: Enhanced teams provide advantages in PvP and challenging
endgame content.

Hi Kristi,
Please accept my apologies for the delay in getting these back to you! The end of the semester was a bit of a whirlwind, but I have some great updates regarding the project.
Right at the end of the semester, we finished the prototype, and the B-360 team visited the Universities at Shady Grove to test it out. It was a huge successthey tried VR for the first time and loved it! One of our biggest wins was that they experienced zero motion sickness, which is a common hurdle in VR development.


Regarding the hardware: We deliberately optimized the engineering so the game would run smoothly on accessible mobile VR (using standard ~$18 smartphone headsets) rather than requiring expensive equipment. This was a key design choice to ensure B-360 can easily deploy this experience to as many students/young riders as possible in the future.
Please find attached to this email:
  1. Screenshots/Images of the game as requested.
  2. My End of Semester Reflection PDF: I cleared sharing this with Dr. Cahn. It provides a deeper dive into our development process. 
If it helps with your story, here are a few key takeaways from the reflection:
  • On the goal: "It's not just about what is funit's about what is useful, accessible, and meaningful for the group you are serving. While still being fun!"
  • On the impact: "Their excitement when they first playtested it truly motivated me and my team and reminded us how our hard work makes a difference."

I would also like to add that the other two groups in our class were full of talent, and it was great to see how all of us approached our visions to cater to the client.


Thank you once again for your patience! I am available if you have any further questions or need more information. 
I hope you have a wonderful holiday break!

Kind Regards,
Georgi Tsvetanski
